Fascinating Facts About Egyptian Pyramids

Age and Construction

The Great Pyramid of Giza was built around 2560 BCE and remained the tallest man-made structure for over 3,800 years.

Perfect Alignment

The Great Pyramid is aligned with true north with an accuracy of 0.15 degrees, showing incredible ancient engineering precision.

Massive Scale

The Great Pyramid was originally 481 feet tall and contains approximately 2.3 million stone blocks, each weighing 2.5 to 15 tons.

Construction Mystery

Despite centuries of research, archaeologists still debate exactly how the ancient Egyptians constructed these massive monuments.

Temperature Control

The pyramids maintain a constant temperature of 68 degrees Fahrenheit (20 degrees Celsius) inside, matching the earth's internal temperature.

Original Appearance

The pyramids were originally covered in white limestone casing stones that made them shine brilliantly in the sun.
