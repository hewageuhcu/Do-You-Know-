Fascinating Facts About Egyptian Pyramids

Do you know? The past Egyptians were like magicians! They built up the great pyramids. Here's something you can add to your kmnowladge bank about Egypt Pyrmaids!

Age and Construction

The Great Pyramid of Giza was built around 2560 BCE and remained the tallest man-made structure for over 3,800 years.

Perfect Alignment

The Great Pyramid is aligned with true north with an accuracy of 0.15 degrees, showing incredible ancient engineering precision.

Massive Scale

The Great Pyramid was originally 481 feet tall and contains approximately 2.3 million stone blocks, each weighing 2.5 to 15 tons.

Construction Mystery

Despite centuries of research, archaeologists still debate exactly how the ancient Egyptians constructed these massive monuments.

Temperature Control

The pyramids maintain a constant temperature of 68 degrees Fahrenheit (20 degrees Celsius) inside, matching the earth's internal temperature.

Original Appearance

The pyramids were originally covered in white limestone casing stones that made them shine brilliantly in the sun.

Khufu (Cheops)

The Great Pyramid was built for Pharaoh Khufu of the Fourth Dynasty. He ruled around 2589-2566 BCE and was known for launching Egypt's most ambitious pyramid-building project.

Khafre

Khafre, son of Khufu, built the second-largest pyramid at Giza. His pyramid appears taller than his father's because it stands on higher ground. He also built the Great Sphinx.

Menkaure

The smallest of the three main Giza pyramids was built by Menkaure, Khafre's son. Though smaller, it features more complex decorative elements and chambers.

Djoser

Before the Giza pyramids, Pharaoh Djoser built the Step Pyramid at Saqqara around 2630 BCE. This was the first pyramid ever built and marked the beginning of the Pyramid Age.
